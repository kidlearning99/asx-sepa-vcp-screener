📚 Strategy Guide — How Each Indicator Works
The complete framework for identifying the highest-probability ASX breakout setups

SEPA — Stage 2 Trend Template
MA50 > MA150 > MA200 + volume + 52W position
Minervini's SEPA (Specific Entry Point Analysis) filters stocks to only those in a genuine Stage 2 primary uptrend. All 7 criteria must align simultaneously — this eliminates 90%+ of stocks and leaves only the highest-probability setups. Stocks with SEPA 6-7/7 have historically produced the market's biggest winners.
› 7/7 = Maximum conviction — act immediately
› 5-6/7 = High conviction — buy on breakout
› 3-4/7 = Developing — add to watchlist
📊
Moving Average Stack
Price > MA50 > MA150 > MA200 all rising
When all four levels are stacked and rising, institutional money is firmly in control across every timeframe. The MA50 is the heartbeat of Stage 2 — healthy stocks bounce off it repeatedly. Breaking below MA50 on heavy volume is an early warning to exit. The MA200 rising for 1+ months confirms the primary trend is healthy.
› Price > MA50 > MA150 > MA200 = Stage 2 confirmed
› MA50 acting as support = institutional accumulation
› Price below MA50 = exit signal, wait for repair
🌀
VCP — Volatility Contraction Pattern
Progressively tighter price swings, volume drying up
VCP is the 'coiling spring' before a breakout. Each successive pullback is shallower than the last, with volume declining — indicating that supply is being absorbed. Institutions accumulate quietly during VCP formation. A 4-contraction VCP with volume at 6-month lows is Minervini's ideal setup. The tighter and longer the base, the more powerful the eventual breakout.
› 4/4 = Textbook base, volume at lows — prime entry
› 3/4 = Good VCP — set alert at pivot high
› 1-2/4 = Early stage — monitor weekly
🔊
Breakout Volume
Today's volume ≥ 1.5× the 50-day average
Volume is the fuel of a breakout. A price move above a pivot high on 1.5x–3x+ normal volume confirms that institutions are aggressively buying. Without this confirmation, the breakout is suspect and likely to fail. The best breakouts see 2x+ volume on the breakout day, with continued above-average volume in the following 2-3 weeks.
› 3x+ avg = Explosive breakout — immediate priority
› 1.5-3x avg = Confirmed breakout — actionable entry
› < 1.5x avg = Weak — wait for volume confirmation
⚡
PVR — Price Volume Ratio
Price move efficiency per unit of volume
PVR reveals how efficiently a stock moves in price relative to the volume required to move it. A high PVR is the fingerprint of institutional accumulation — smart money buys in size but manages orders carefully to avoid moving the price too much. PVR > 1.5 means the stock is rising on relatively modest volume effort, signaling strong latent demand.
› PVR > 1.5 = Strong accumulation signature
› PVR 1.0–1.5 = Healthy demand / balanced
› PVR < 0.5 = Distribution — heavy selling
📍
52-Week High/Low Position
Within 25% of 52W high + 25%+ above 52W low
Minervini's research proves that the biggest winners are bought near their 52-week highs — NOT when they're 'cheap' and beaten down. Stocks near all-time highs are strong for a reason: institutional sponsorship. At the same time, the stock must be 25%+ above its 52-week low, confirming it has already made a meaningful Stage 2 recovery.
› Within 5-15% of 52W high = ideal buy zone
› 25%+ above 52W low = confirmed Stage 2
› > 25% below 52W high = outside buy zone — avoid
📈
200-Day MA Trend
200-day MA must be rising for ≥ 1 month
The 200-day MA is the market's long-term judge. When it's rising, the stock is in a primary bull trend — institutions are supporting price on every dip. A flat or declining 200-day MA means the stock is in Stage 1 (basing) or Stage 3/4 (distribution/decline). Never buy a stock with a declining 200-day MA regardless of how good everything else looks.
› 200D MA rising = Stage 2 primary uptrend
› 200D MA flat = Stage 1 base-building — patience
› 200D MA falling = Stage 3/4 — no entry
🎯
SEPA Score (0–7)
Combined conviction rating across all 7 criteria
The SEPA score is your single most important number. It combines all 7 criteria into a unified conviction rating. Focus exclusively on stocks scoring 5-7/7 — these represent the rare intersection of technical momentum, fundamental strength, and institutional interest. Lower-scoring stocks will always be more abundant but far less productive.
› 6-7/7 = Rare — maximum conviction, act on breakout
› 4-5/7 = Good setup — watchlist + price alert
› < 4/7 = Avoid — too many criteria unmet
